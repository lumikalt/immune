#+title: Medical Testing

Medical tests help report the presence or absence of a medical condition.

An individual with the condition is a "positive", and one without it is a "negative".

A medical test, therefore, can be measured in terms of its [[id:534f3093-d793-4240-9dbb-d7fe096e3dd1][sensitivity]] and [[id:55b092e6-9eb3-414b-aabd-62306b2b4d7f][specificity]].
Note that, if the true status of the condition cannot be known, these measures can be defined relative to a [[id:58f62b40-c612-47a4-9819-6b3bc21993f5][gold standard test]], which is assumed correct.

For all testing, both diagnoses and screening, there is a trade-off between sensitivity and specificity, as typically higher sensitivity means lower specificity, and vice versa.
